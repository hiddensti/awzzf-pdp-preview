Why Flow-Form

The middle path between cast and forged — without the compromises.

Flow-forming starts with a cast wheel and spins the rim under heat and pressure. The aluminum compresses and aligns, the rim densifies, and we use less material for the same strength. Lighter wheel, stronger rim, price that doesn't scare anyone off.

Lighter than cast

Less unsprung weight — quicker turn-in, better acceleration, improved range on EVs. Densification during flow-forming lets us cut material while keeping the same load rating.

Stronger on the rim

Spin-forming aligns the aluminum grain along the rim — the part that takes pothole and curb impact. Fatigue resistance closer to forged at a flow-form price.

AW QC, AW warranty

Same testing, same fitment library, same Lifetime Structural Warranty as forged AWZZ. Hub-centric fit, AW center cap, TPMS-ready bore.

FAQ

Pre-order, finishes, fit

What is flow-forming, in plain English?
A flow-formed wheel starts as a cast blank, then spins on a mandrel while rollers press the rim section under heat. The aluminum grain stretches and compresses, the rim becomes denser and stronger than the original cast, and we end up using less material to hit the same load rating. Net effect: lighter wheel, stronger rim, price between cast and forged.
How does AWZZF compare to AWZZ in real driving?
AWZZ (forged) is still the lightest and stiffest wheel we make — it's the choice for a track car or someone who hits curbs hard. AWZZF closes most of the gap for a daily-driven Model 3/Y while costing meaningfully less. If your priorities are looks, daily comfort, and not breaking the bank, AWZZF is the right call.
What's the warranty?
Lifetime Structural Warranty against manufacturing defects, plus our 50%-off accidental-damage replacement program. Same coverage as the forged AWZZ line.
Will my finish chip or fade?
All four finishes are powder-coated and clear-coated. Normal driving care and standard wheel-safe cleaners (no acid wheel cleaners) will keep them looking factory for years. Pothole or curb damage is covered by the 50% replacement program, not the structural warranty.
Can I order a custom size or finish?
For non-standard sizes or finishes outside the four shown, contact us — we run custom orders through a 9-step factory process with full prepayment. Lead time is typically longer than the AWZZF pre-order window.
